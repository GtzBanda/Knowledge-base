# Live Performance Tips

![Live Performance](../images/live-performance.jpg)

## Overview

Playing live is very different from practicing at home. Performing in front of an audience requires preparation, confidence, and the ability to adapt if something unexpected happens. While mistakes can occur during any performance, being well prepared helps musicians stay focused and enjoy the experience.

Before a performance, musicians should make sure their instruments are tuned, cables are working properly, and all equipment has been tested. Arriving early also provides enough time to set up, complete a sound check, and solve any problems before the audience arrives. Good preparation allows performers to concentrate on the music instead of worrying about technical issues.

## Tips for a Successful Performance

Before stepping on stage, remember to:

- Tune your instrument before every performance.
- Bring extra picks, strings, cables, and batteries.
- Test your equipment during sound check.
- Practice your songs until you feel confident.
- Stay relaxed and focus on connecting with the audience.

## During the Performance

A successful performance is about more than playing the correct notes. Musicians should stay engaged with the audience, listen to the other performers, and remain calm if a mistake happens. Most audiences never notice small errors, but they do remember performers who play with confidence and energy.

> "Preparation builds confidence, and confidence leads to better performances."
